Seven Essential Capabilities for SaaS Security

Comprehensive SSPM Program Requirements

A comprehensive SSPM program and toolset must deliver seven essential capabilities that directly address the SaaS security risks we identified. Each capability targets specific risk scenarios:

1

üîç Discovery & Inventory

Addresses: Shadow SaaS & App Sprawl

What it does:

Discovery of SaaS usage and connected apps via logs, API connectors, and identity telemetry with risk rating of applications.

How it helps:

Eliminates blind spots in your SaaS estate, providing visibility into unauthorized applications before they become security incidents.

2

‚öôÔ∏è API-Based Configuration Assessments

Addresses: Misconfiguration & Drift

What it does:

Continuous configuration assessments for major SaaS platforms with benchmarks and actionable recommendations, appearing in Security Exposure Management and Secure Score.

How it helps:

Prevents security incidents by identifying and correcting misconfigurations before attackers can exploit them.

3

üîó OAuth Governance & Attack Path Awareness

Addresses: Supply Chain & Lateral Movement

What it does:

Surfaces high-privilege OAuth apps and shows how they can be used in lateral movement, including them in exposure and attack-path analytics.

How it helps:

Prevents supply chain attacks by identifying risky app integrations and their potential attack paths across your SaaS ecosystem.

4

‚ö° Real-Time Enforcement

Addresses: Data Exfiltration & Policy Violations

What it does:

Session controls, conditional access app control, DLP, and anomaly detection for sanctioned apps plus network-level enforcement to block unsanctioned apps.

How it helps:

Stops data exfiltration in real-time by enforcing consistent security policies across all SaaS applications.

5

üë§ Identity-Driven Controls

Addresses: Access Risk & Privilege Escalation

What it does:

User/device risk assessment, CA policies, PIM, and access reviews to keep posture aligned with least privilege principles.

How it helps:

Reduces blast radius by ensuring users have only the minimum necessary access to SaaS applications and data.

6

ü§ñ Automation & SOAR

Addresses: Response Time & Scale

What it does:

Continuous monitoring, alerting, and automated remediation including auto-block, auto-label, OAuth revocation, and session/device isolation.

How it helps:

Enables instant response to threats at scale, reducing mean time to remediation from hours to seconds.

7

üåê Unified SecOps & Exposure Management

What it does:

Unified portal and data model to prioritize SSPM findings alongside endpoint, identity, cloud, and external attack surface management.

Eliminates tool sprawl and context switching, enabling security teams to manage all risks from a single pane of glass.
